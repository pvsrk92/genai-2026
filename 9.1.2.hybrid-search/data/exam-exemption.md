# Exemptions

## What is an exemption?

An exemption is an exception from a current rule. If you are unable to observe regulations, requirements or deadlines, you can apply for an exemption. You can find all the rules for your study programme here on my.cbs.dk and in [your programme regulations].

Examples of typical exemptions:

-   Extension of enrolment
-   Withdrawal from courses
-   Withdrawal from 1st exam attempt
-   Extra exam attempt
-   [Extended exam time]
-   Submission deadlines
-   [Special conditions at exams]
-   [Leave of absence]
-   [Maternity and paternity leave]

## When are exemptions granted?

In order to receive an exemption there have to be unusual circumstances or you have to belong to a group of students that typically have difficulties studying at a normal pace or following all the rules.

An application for exemption is always based on your own specific situation, and therefore always processed individually. As such, it is not possible to specify the exact circumstances that will be sufficient grounds for you to get an exemption.

It also means that you are not automatically entitled to an exemption just because someone else has been granted an exemption from the same rule.

**Please note:** It can take up to 8 weeks from the time you send in your application until you receive an answer. The processing time may be longer during the summer, because many employees take their vacation in July.

## Examples of special circumstances

Special circumstances are, generally speaking, external and unplanned, and not something, you could have planned for or taken into consideration beforehand. They are things that happen to an individual, and, typically, not a lot of students at once.

If you have questions about your possibilities for exemption, or if you need to talk about your situation before you apply, you are welcome to contact [a student guidance counsellor].

#### Special circumstances CAN include e.g.:

-   illness suffered by you or your immediate family
-   death in the immediate family or social circle
-   special personal circumstances
-   [Maternity or paternity leave]

#### Special circumstances do generally NOT include e.g.:

-   having a job
-   exchange and internships
-   lack of knowledge of the rules
-   oversight or delay
-   international students unaccustomed to Danish academic culture

## How to apply for an exemption

You apply for an exemption on [exemption.cbs.dk](https://exemption.cbs.dk/).

You can find guides on [Guide to exemption.cbs.dk].

## Before you apply

Your application will of course be treated in confidence. Some cases will be processed by a caseworker, other cases by a study board, programme director or programme manager. They are all required to maintain confidentiality. When you apply, you have the option to indicate that your uploaded documentation is confidential.

If you have a specific request for your application to be made entirely anonymous before it is presented to the study board, you can write this in your application.

## Application deadline

A study board, programme director or programme manager, depending on the type of exemption, will process applications for exemption.

The application deadline also depends on who is processing your application.

If your application is processed by your study board, the application deadline is typically 1-2 weeks before the study board meeting. If you submit your application after the deadline, your application will not be treated until the next meeting.

You can find the application deadline under [Study board meetings].

#### Application deadlines for exams

If you apply for an exemption in relation to an exam, e.g. extended exam time, extra exam attempt, or submission deadline, we recommend that you apply well in advance, so you will receive a reply before the exam is held.

Different deadlines apply depending on your course:

-   Mandatory courses and electives: the application deadline is **6 weeks before the exam** is held.
-   Courses at Summer University: the application deadline is **3 weeks before the exam** is held.
-   Note specific deadlines on 24 February and 24 September if you apply for special conditions at exams/extended exam time.

## Answer

We do our best to process you application as quickly as possible.

Exemptions that are processed by the study boards will typically be discussed at a study board meeting and then further processed. Therefore, it may take up to 6 weeks from the time you submit your application before you receive an answer with the decision.

Processing times may be longer if your application or documentation is insufficient and the caseworker needs to obtain more information from you.

You will receive the decision on exemption.cbs.dk with a notification on your CBS email.

## Complaints

If your application is rejected, or partially rejected, you may have the option of complaining over the decision. In the answer you receive, there will be information on how, and under which circumstances, you can complain over the decision.

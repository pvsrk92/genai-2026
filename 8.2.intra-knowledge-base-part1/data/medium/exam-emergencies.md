# Exam emergencies

Unforeseen things can happen in connection with your exams. Here you can read about the most typical problems, and what you can do if it happens to you.

__

You are late for a written sit-in exam

For written sit-in exams you must be present in the exam room no later than 15 minutes before the exam starts, at which time the doors are closed. If you arrive later you may be refused admission. Approx. 20 minutes after the exam has started, the doors will be opened briefly and you can join the exam if you have arrived late. You will not be given extra time for the exam if you have arrived late. If you arrive after this time, you will be refused admission entirely. You will be registered as absent and use one exam attempt.

__

You are late for an oral exam

For oral exams you must arrive early to avoid unnecessary breaks between individual examinations. If other students are absent, the examiners will skip ahead to the next student on the list. It is your responsibility to be present when it is your turn, even if that is earlier than planned. If you are not present when it is your turn, you may be refused.

__

You submit a paper past deadline

If you submit a paper past deadline, it will be rejected. The deadline for submission appears at Digital Exam. If you find yourself in an unexpected situation that have delayed you, you can apply for an exemption for a late submission.

 Read more under [Submission]

__

You fall ill at an exam

If you fall ill at an exam, you should give notice of your illness and have your exam stopped. If you do not give notice of your illness, but instead submit a blank, you will have participated in the exam, and you will receive the grade -3 and use an exam attempt.

If you give notice of your illness, your exam will be stopped and you will not use an exam attempt.

You can read more about how to withdraw due to illness under [Illness during exams].

__

You submit a blank

If you submit a blank at the exam, you will receive the grade -3. Whether you submit a blank or fail to show up for an exam, you will have used one exam attempt and you will automatically be registered for the next exam attempt if you have more exam attempts left. However, this does not apply if you have passed the course with an overall grade point average.

__

You fail to show up for a written exam

If you fail to show up for a written sit-in exam or fail to submit a written home assignment, you will receive the result U (absent), and will have used an exam attempt. If you have already accessed, opened or downloaded a given assignment, you will have started the exam and you will receive the grade -3 if you do not submit. You will automatically be registered for the next exam attempt if you have more attempts left. However, this does not apply if you have passed the course with an overall grade point average.

__

You fail to show up for an oral exam

If you fail to show up for an oral exam without a written product, you will receive the result U (absent), and you will have used an exam attempt. You will automatically be registered for the next exam attempt (if you have more exam attempts left).

If you fail to show up for an oral exam based on a written product that you have submitted, only the written product will be included in the assessment. You will receive the grade -3, and you will have used an exam attempt. How a re-exam will be conducted, depends on your programme and the specific course. In the course description, you can see what applies for the re-exam in the specific course.

__

Technical problems

If you experience technical problems when trying to submit an exam paper digitally, you must contact the [IT-support] as soon as possible. If technical problems arise which affect several students, you will be informed via email of what to do and of whether there will be any changes to your exam.

__

Your name does not appear on the exam list

If you are registered for an exam and are entitled to take the exam, but your name does not appear from the published exam list, you must contact us via the [Exams Helpdesk](https://helpdesk.cbs.dk/SC/ServiceCatalog/RequestOffering/c221e2f8-ba4e-c44a-0aff-b24750497b0e%2c31dbbe8a-a3d8-030d-e1ee-7cb89fa19fa8).

-   Log on to the [Exams Helpdesk](https://helpdesk.cbs.dk/SC/ServiceCatalog/RequestOffering/c221e2f8-ba4e-c44a-0aff-b24750497b0e%2c31dbbe8a-a3d8-030d-e1ee-7cb89fa19fa8) using your CBS username and password.
-   Choose _Something else_ in the first drop-down menu
-   Find your course in the second drop-down menu. You can search for your course either by name or course number
-   Under 'Message' describe your situation
-   Press 'Save'. Your case is now ready for processing. You will receive an email from the helpdesk confirming your case creation.
-   Within 7 working days you will receive an answer in the helpdesk. You will get an email from the helpdesk whenever there are updates to your case.

You will then be added to the exam list.

__

Your exam is cancelled

You must participate in the exam at the date and time stated. You cannot choose a different date or time. Nor is it possible to switch a exam date or time with a fellow student. 

Exception: If you have two exams scheduled for the same time, you can contact [exams@cbs.dk](mailto:exams@cbs.dk) for help to solve the issue.

**Exams at Summer University:** As the Exam schedule for exams at Summer University is announced before the application process, it is the your responsibility to check the schedule before you apply for courses. Therefore no special arrangements are made when you have two exams scheduled for the same time.

__

You do not pass

If you do not pass an exam, you will automatically be registered for the next exam attempt. As a general rule you have 3 attempts in total to pass each exam.

Read more about [registration for exams]

__

You want a different exam date

You must participate in the exam at the date and time stated. You cannot choose a different date or time. Nor is it possible to switch an exam date or time with a fellow student. 

Exception: If you have two exams scheduled for the same time, you can contact us via the [Exams Helpdesk](https://helpdesk.cbs.dk/SC/ServiceCatalog/RequestOffering/c221e2f8-ba4e-c44a-0aff-b24750497b0e%2c31dbbe8a-a3d8-030d-e1ee-7cb89fa19fa8) for help to solve the issue.

-   Log on to the [Exams Helpdesk](https://helpdesk.cbs.dk/SC/ServiceCatalog/RequestOffering/c221e2f8-ba4e-c44a-0aff-b24750497b0e%2c31dbbe8a-a3d8-030d-e1ee-7cb89fa19fa8) using your CBS username and password.
-   Choose _Exam overlap_ in the first drop-down menu
-   Find your course in the second drop-down menu. You can search for your course either by name or course number
-   Under 'Message' describe your situation
-   Attach the necessary documentation under 'Attach documentation'
-   Press 'Save'. Your case is now ready for processing. You will receive an email from the helpdesk confirming your case creation.
-   Within 7 working days you will receive an answer in the helpdesk. You will get an email from the helpdesk whenever there are updates to your case.

**Exams at Summer University:** As the Exam schedule for exams at Summer University is announced before the application process, it is your responsibility to check the schedule before you apply for courses. Therefore no special arrangements are made when you have two exams scheduled for the same time.

## Exam dates for oral exams

We aim to publish the specific exam times as early as possible, so you have the opportunity to plan your semester.

#### For oral exams with a written product

Until your submission deadline, the exam schedule is tentative. This means that your exam date is fixed, but the exact time is preliminary and may change after you submit the written product.

#### For oral exams without a written product

For oral exams without a written product, the exam schedule is generally final. However, the exact time may change in case of prior withdrawal of registration, absences, or failure to meet mandatory activity requirements. Therefore, check the exam schedule before your exam and show up in good time according to the rules.

If you are unsure about what a written product covers, you can read about [the different types of oral examinations here].

#### Exam dates are determined by CBS

You cannot request a specific date or time for your exam, nor can you swap places with another student.

Read more about the rules for arrival times under [Rules for oral exams].

## Time and exam order for oral exams

You can find the specific date and place for your oral exams on [Digital Exam](https://eksamen.cbs.dk/).

Your exam time will be shown either as a number in an order or a specific starting time for your exam.

#### Without a written product

For oral exams where you have not submitted a written product, you will be given a number on the list. It looks like this in Digital Exam:

Example: Number in a row (oral sit-in exam)

You will know the time of your exam from the number of minutes per student, compared to your number in the order.

You must show up well in time for your exam, and no later than:

-   the time of the day the exam is scheduled to start, if the time of your exam is before 13:00. See when your exam starts is on [Digital Exam](https://eksamen.cbs.dk/)
-   11:00 if the time of your exam is after 13:00


#### With a written product

For oral exams, where you have submitted a written product, you will have a starting time. It looks like this in Digital Exam:

Example: Specific starting time (oral exam on the basis of project assignment)


If you have a specific time for your exam, you must show up at least 2 hours before. Meanwhile, if you are one of the first students, you must simply show up well ahead of time for your exam.


## Be ready well ahead of time - changes may occur

The order for the exam is only a guideline. There is a risk of changes occurring on the day of the exam itself, e.g. due to delays or a student not showing up for the exam.

This could mean that your exam will either be earlier or later than expected. It is your responsibility to be ready at the examination room when your name is called. If you are not present, you might risk using an exam attempt.

## What is an exam overlap?

An exam overlap is when two exams are scheduled in the same period of time. Overlapping exams can be written sit-in exams, oral exams, or time-limited home assignments with a duration of either 24, 48, or 72 hours.

Not all cases where two exams are scheduled for the same day will be considered exam overlaps. Therefore, it might not be possible to change the exam dates in all cases.

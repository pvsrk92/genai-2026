# Deadlines and complaints procedure

## Deadline for exam complaints

Your exam complaint must reach Legal no later than:

-   Oral exams: 2 weeks after the date of the exam.
-   Written sit-in exams: 2 weeks after the assessment deadline, i.e. 28 days after the submission deadline + 2 weeks.
-   Written home assignment: 2 weeks after the assessment deadline, i.e. 28 days after the submission deadline + 2 weeks.

## Postponing the deadline for complaints

You can apply for a postponement of the complaint deadline if you are unable to meet the deadline due to extraordinary circumstances.

You can apply for a postponement of the deadline for complaints by sending an email from your own CBS email to [eksamensklager@cbs.dk](mailto:eksamensklager@cbs.dk).

**Please note** that you must apply for the postponement before the complaint deadline expires.

## Case processing time

You can expect the case processing time for your complaint to be up to 10-12 weeks.

## If you did not pass the exam that you are complaining about

If you did not pass the exam you are complaining about, you will automatically be registered for the next exam attempt. Because it may take 10-12 weeks before you receive a decision, we recommend that you participate in the exam, otherwise you will have used one exam attempt.

If you pass the exam before a decision has been reached about your complaint, you can withdraw your complaint.

## You participate in the retake while your complaint is pending

If you choose to take a retake exam while your complaint is being processed there are the following scenarios:

#### Your complaint is upheld

If your complaint is upheld, you can choose between the following options:

-   You accept the offer of a re-assessment. The grade from the re-exam is annulled and the result of the re-assessment applies.

-   You accept the offer of a new exam. The grade from the re-exam is annulled and the result of the new exam applies.

-   You refuse the offer of a re-assessment or new exam. The grading from the re-exam will apply.


#### Your complaint is not upheld

 If your appeal is not upheld the grading from the re-exam will apply.

## Withdrawing a complaint

You can withdraw your complaint before a final decision is made. Once a decision is made, you can no longer withdraw your complaint. You can withdraw your complaint by sending an email from your own CBS email to:

-   Complaints about assessment: [eksamensklager@cbs.dk](mailto:eksamensklager@cbs.dk)
-   Complaints about other issues at the exam: [legal@cbs.dk](mailto:legal@cbs.dk)

# Before you make a complaint

Cheating and exam complaints

Before you make a complaint

Should students feel they have been wrongly treated in connection with teaching, supervision or examination, they are entitled to file a complaint.

## Different types of complaints

There are different types of complaints, depending on what your complaint concerns, and they are processed either by Legal or your study board.

Make sure you are clear on what you are complaining about, so you can submit your complaint with the correct unit.

-   **Teaching, lecturer or supervision:** If you are dissatisfied with the teaching you receive or with your lecturer, you can file a complaint with your study board. Read more about [co](/Undergraduate/Pages/Complaints-about-the-teaching.aspx)[mplaints about teaching].
-   **Exam:** If you are unhappy with your assessment, the examination itself or the examination basis, you can file a complaint with Legal.

#### Example

If you want to file a complaint about supervision, examination and assessment, you must write 2 complaints:

-   One complaint about the supervision you have received, which must be submitted with your study board, **AND**
-   One complaint about your examination and assessment, which must be submitted with Legal

## Exam complaints

You should consider several things before you decide to file a complaint.

Think about:

-   why you want to file a complaint
-   what exactly you want to complain about
-   what you want to achieve with your complaint

You can read more below.

**Please note:** It may take 10-12 weeks before you receive a decision. It may therefore affect your further studies. Read more about [Deadlines and complaints procedure]

## Why do you want to file a complaint?

First consider why you want to file a complaint, and whether there could be a better solution for you than a complaint. For example, if you file a complaint about your grade because you do not understand why you have received this grade assessment, perhaps it would be a better solution to contact your examiner for an explanation.

## What do you want to complain about?

Make sure you are clear on what you want to complain about, as there can be different outcomes for your complaint depending on what it concerns.

Do you want to complain about:

-   The assessment/grading
-   Other issues at the exam, e.g. examination, exam conditions or exam paper.
-   Teaching, lecturer or supervision. Read more under [Complaints about the teaching].

You can file a complaint about several issues if you want, but you should separate the issues in your complaint. Read more under [How to make a complaint].

## Read the learning objectives

If you want to file a complaint about the assessment, you should have academic arguments for why your performance was not assessed correctly. Therefore, you should thoroughly study the academic requirements for the exam as described under learning objectives in the course description. By comparing your performance with the learning objectives, you can determine whether you in fact have a basis for filing a complaint about your assessment. If there is a grading instruction available for your exam, you can use that too. If so, it will be available on [Canvas](https://canvas.cbs.dk/)

## Contact your examiner

We also recommend that you contact your examiner for an explanation for your assessment. You will normally receive an oral explanation and you cannot claim to have it in writing.

The explanation can help you to understand the assessment and to determine whether you have a basis for filing a complaint. You can find your lecturer's contact information on [Canvas](https://canvas.cbs.dk/)

**Please note:** you must still meet the deadline for complaints even if you are awaiting a response from your examiner. Read more about [Deadlines and complaints procedure](/pages/findingyourpage.aspx?r=pages/deadlines-and-complaints-procedure.aspx).

## My examiner is willing to change my grade

Your examiner cannot change your exam grade. In these cases, you must file a complaint, and your examiner can then make a statement. If you are offered a new assessment or a new exam, new examiners will also be appointed. Your original examiner will then have no influence on whether your grade is changed.

## Possible outcomes of your complaint

Before you file a complaint, you should understand the possible outcomes of your complaint, so you do not risk an outcome that you are not interested in. For example, a complaint about an examination could mean that you will have to take a new examination.

An exam complaint could have the possible outcomes:

-   You are offered a new assessment (re-assessment) with a new examiner. Only applies to written exams.
-   You are offered a new exam (re-exam) with a new examiner.
-   Your complaint is rejected

It is up to you whether you want to accept an offer for a new assessment or a new exam. 


**Note:** If you receive a different grade at a new assessment or new exam, the new grade will replace the original grade – even if it is lower than the original grade. You cannot choose to keep your original grade instead.

Read more about the possible outcomes of complaints under [Ruling and appeal]

# Ruling and appeal

Cheating and exam complaints

Ruling and appeal

You can expect that it will take 10-12 weeks before you receive the final decision.

## How you will receive the decision

When a decision has been made on your case, you will receive an email informing you of the decision.

## Possible outcomes

The complaint may result in the following decisions:

-   You are offered a new assessment (re-assessment) with a new examiner. Only applies to written exams.
-   You are offered a new exam (re-exam) with a new examiner.
-   Your complaint is rejected


The decision you receive will explain what you should do if you are offered a new assessment or a new exam.

## How to respond to the decision

If you are offered a new exam or a new assessment, you must consider whether to accept this offer.

Please note the following before you make your decision:

-   _New grade:_ If you receive a different grade at a new assessment or new exam, the new grade will replace the original grade –  even if it is lower than the original grade. You cannot choose to keep your original grade instead. If you decline the offer of a new assessment or new exam, you will keep your original grade.
-   _New examiners:_ There will be new examiners for a new assessment or a new exam.
-   You cannot file a complaint about or appeal the new assessment (unless it concerns the new exam conditions, the new examination or the new exam paper).


If you choose to accept the offer of a new exam or a new assessment, you must inform Legal no later than 2 weeks after you receive the decision. You can do so by sending an email to [eksamensklager@cbs.dk](mailto:eksamensklager@cbs.dk) in which you state that you want to accept the offer. If you do not reply within 2 weeks, the offer lapses and your complaint is closed.

## If your complaint is rejected – appeals 

If you disagree with the decision, you can appeal it within 2 weeks after you have received it.

## Who will process the appeal?

The Board of Appeal Secretariat under CBS Legal will process the appeal. They will ensure that the case is brought before a board of appeal which consists of two external examiners, a lecturer and a student in the field. It will usually be a lecturer and a student from the relevant study board.

## How to appeal

You can send appeals and legal complaints

-   By email: [legal@cbs.dk](mailto:legal@cbs.dk)
-   By post: CBS Legal, Solbjerg Plads 3 - C1., 2000 Frederiksberg.

## Possible outcomes for appeals

The appeal may result in the following decisions:

-   You are offered a new assessment (re-assessment) with (a) new examiner(s). Only applies to written exams.
-   You are offered a new exam (re-exam) with (a) new examiner(s).
-   Your complaint is rejected.


The decision you receive will explain what you should do if you are offered a new assessment or a new exam.

Please note the following before you make your decision:

-   _New grade:_ If you receive a different grade at a new assessment or new exam, the new grade will replace the original grade –  even if it is lower than the original grade. You cannot choose to keep your original grade instead. If you decline the offer of a new assessment or new exam, you will keep your original grade.
-   _New examiners:_ There will be new examiners for a new assessment or a new exam.
-   You cannot file a complaint about or appeal the new assessment (unless it concerns the new exam conditions, the new examination or the new exam paper).


If you choose to accept the offer of a new exam or a new assessment, you must inform Legal no later than 2 weeks after you receive the decision. You can do so by sending an email to [legal@cbs.dk](mailto:legal@cbs.dk) in which you state that you want to accept the offer. If you do not reply within 2 weeks, the offer lapses and your appeal is closed.

# Complaints about the teaching

Courses and teaching

Complaints about the teaching

## Why are you dissatisfied with the course?

You may find that the teaching you receive can vary. However, this is not necessarily because of the lecturer or the teaching activity itself. So before you file a complaint, consider if your experience could in fact match one of these examples:

-   **You feel you are not learning anything**: We are not always aware of learning something new while we are in the process of learning. Sometimes we realise later that we did in fact learn something. So give the course a chance.
-   **You find it hard to keep up in a course**, simply because the subject is difficult. You cannot expect to pick everything up easily, and sometimes learning will take an extra effort from you.
-   **You find a course irrelevant** compared to the other courses. Your programme's structure is carefully prepared and continuously developed by your study board. All courses are a part of a greater academic context that you will probably not fully understand until you have completed the entire programme. You can read about the programme structure and contexts in your programme regulations.
-   **You are dissatisfied with your books**: Not all textbooks are 'user friendly'. Your lecturer will always try to choose the best possible teaching material. You can ask your lecturer why this exact book is part of the curriculum.
-   **You dislike your lecturer**: Lecturers differ and so do their approaches to teaching. Perhaps you dislike your lecturer's style and personality. But the relationship between lecturer and student is a professional one, and you do not have to like your lecturers in order to learn something from them.
-   **Student behaviour during class**: The atmosphere in class very much influences how well the teaching works, and therefore both the lecturer and the students are responsible for the teaching activities. It is disruptive when students are late for class, leave early, are unprepared, are not actively participating, surf the web during class or talk (when they are not supposed to). So consider your own behaviour and that of your fellow students.

## Before you file a complaint

Before you file a complaint, consider what you want to achieve. Note that it may take up to 6 weeks before your study board has reviewed your complaint, and only then can the study board make any necessary changes.

Perhaps there are other and better ways to achieve what you want. And perhaps a talk with your lecturer is all it takes. Before you file a complaint, you should:

-   **talk to your fellow students**: If they agree with you, you can discuss what you all want to do to improve the teaching.
-   **talk to your lecturer**: Your lecturers can explain to you why they teach in a certain way or they can make changes. If you have specific wishes or suggestions for improvements, let your lecturers know so they have an opportunity to respond. You can choose to take it up in class with your fellow students, or use your lecturer's office hours. In either case, remember to be factual and fair, and to have a constructive approach to possible solutions.
-   **contact the course coordinator**: If speaking to your lecturer does not help, or if your dissatisfaction concerns issues that are beyond your lecturer's control, you can contact the course coordinator, who has the academic responsibility for the course. The course coordinator appears from the course description.
-   **contact the quality board**: You can also contact your programme's Quality Board

## Remember to evaluate your courses!

Your study board will use the evaluations to develop and improve the course and the programme for you and your fellow students.

Read more about [course evaluations]

## How to file a complaint

If the abovementioned courses of action have not helped, you can file a complaint with your study board. If you are several students or a whole class who agrees, the complaint will probably have more weight than if only a single student is dissatisfied. So always make sure to talk with your fellow students first.

The complaint must be addressed to your study board. It must explain and elaborate on the elements of the teaching that you find unsatisfactory. Your arguments must be reasoned and fair. Describing your lecturer or his/her teaching in a derogatory way will not be to your benefit. Also describe what you would like to achieve with your complaint. This will help the study board review your complaint.

## Complaints about Summer University

## Outcome of complaints about the teaching

A complaint about the teaching may have the following outcomes:

-   The student(s) complaint will not be upheld
-   The class will continue to be taught by the same lecturer.
-   The class will receive additional lessons
-   The class will be given a new lecturer (rarely happens after the teaching has started)

You can expect that it may take up to 6 weeks for the study board to review your complaint, because it will be reviewed at a study board meeting. You can find the dates for the study board meetings and the deadline for submitting cases to the individual meetings under Study board meetings.

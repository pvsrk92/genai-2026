# Cooperation with companies

## Finding a company

Finding a company or an organisation to cooperate with can take time. You should therefore start your search well in advance, before you really need the data.

You do not necessarily need to choose a company. You can also choose to cooperate with an NGO, sports club, trade union, political party, and other types of organisations.

You can find a company in many ways e.g.:

-   **Start by searching your network:** And networks you know through e.g. friends or family. If you know someone, who knows someone, you will probably save a lot of time.
-   **Contact multiple companies:** Even if you will only need one company, you should consider contacting multiple as you cannot expect a positive response from the first company you contact.
-   **Make a broad search for possible companies**: The most well known organisations receive many inquiries from students, and they cannot agree to everyone.
-   **Choose your own workplace:** It may be easy for you to get access to data in a company you already know and which knows you. However, it may be difficult take a critical approach and ask silly questions about the company's practice because your co-workers and managers already know you as an employee.
-   **Use** [**CBS' project and jobsite – CareerGate**](https://cbscareergate.jobteaser.com/en%20)**:** In CareerGate, you can find companies and organisations, which are searching for students for certain projects. However, you should thoroughly consider if the project is both interesting and relevant for you and your academic field.



## Contacting the company

Before you contact a company, you should do some research. Then it will be easier for you to aim your inquiry at the specific company and to convince them to choose you and your project.

Tell them the following:

-   About yourself and your academic profile.
-   Why is this specific company relevant to your project?
-   What can you offer this specific company?
-   What kind of resources will it likely require of the company to cooperate with you?

If you inquire via email, write that you will call them within a week. If you do not receive a reply to your email, it is probably because your project does not have as high priority to them as it does to you.

## Cooperation with the company

It will differ how much experience the individual company has with being a case in a student project.

When you begin the cooperation, it is therefore important that you and the company discuss your future cooperative relation and mutual expectations, and not only the project itself.

By using the checklist you reduce the risk of problems arising later on which may make it difficult for you to meet CBS' requirements for your project.

## Confidentiality

If you need access to business sensitive and confidential data, the company will often want your assignment, project or thesis to be treated with confidentiality.

Companies will often demand that you sign an agreement on confidentiality. Before you start the cooperation, you should therefore ensure that you and the company have signed an agreement that both parties can keep.

If you do not make sure that you have a clear agreement, you risk that the company later on demands that you sign a confidentiality agreement before giving you access to (more) data. It may delay your thesis if you cannot reach an agreement, and at worst, it may mean that you will have to end the cooperation and find another company.



**Please note**: if your thesis is delayed because you have not entered into an agreement on confidentiality well in advance, you must still meet the submission deadline for your thesis.

## Handling personal data

If you gain access to or if you collect personal data in connection with your Master Thesis or an assignment, you are responsible for taking care of the data and must not do anything that could damage the data subject.  Read more about what personal data is and how to to comply with the rules for processing personal data below.

# Confidentiality

Assignments and projects

Confidentiality

If you or a company want CBS to treat an assignment, project or thesis with confidentiality, you must:

1.  indicate that the paper is confidential in Digital Exam when you upload it
2.  clearly state on the front page that the paper is confidential

Thus, you do not have to sign a confidentiality agreement to have CBS treat your paper or thesis as confidential.

A confidentiality agreement is a contract between you and a company. You can read more about this below.

## How CBS treats confidential papers

CBS and CBS' employees are subject to confidentiality as stipulated in the Danish Administration Act and will therefore treat your paper as confidential if it appears from Digital Exam and on the paper. This also includes external examiners.

That CBS treats your paper as confidential implies that:

-   only your supervisor, examiner and external examiner will have access to your paper
-   an oral exam will not be open to the public
-   the public will not have access to the paper
-   in case a complaint is lodged against an examination, the employees processing the complaint will be granted access to the paper. This also includes a new supervisor, examiner and/or external examiner if the appeal results in a new exam
-   CBS will file your paper in accordance with the rules on filing.



## Confidentiality and collaboration with companies

Companies will often demand that you sign an agreement on confidentiality before you get access to company-related data and information that they do not wish to be published.

A confidentiality agreement is a contract between you and the company stipulating that you will treat your paper, project or thesis as confidential and thereby not publish information about the company.

The contract does NOT involve CBS but is a contract between you and the company.

**Please note**: if your paper contains sensitive personal data, you must indicate that your paper is confidential on Digital Exam, also if the company has not asked for it.



## Use CBS' template

CBS has made templates for confidentiality agreements. We encourage you to use it when entering into an agreement on confidentiality with a company.

CBS' templates has been prepared by CBS' legal advisors, and they take into account that your paper is to be submitted and assessed at an exam at CBS. Therefore, they do not include any requirements that will be difficult to meet as a student.

As CBS' templates are an agreement you enter into with a company, you should review your agreement thoroughly and actively consider whether you can accept and adhere to the agreement's requirements. Both you and the company must sign the contract.

## The company wants to use its own agreement on confidentiality

Some companies require that you use the company's own confidentiality agreement. As a confidentiality agreement only is an agreement between you and the company, it is your decision to sign the company's agreement.

However, before signing, you should consider whether you can accept the terms of the agreement. If you are in doubt about the terms of the agreement, we recommend that you seek external legal advice. Unfortunately, CBS cannot help you review the contract, as it is a matter between you and the company. We have made a check list you can use before entering into an agreement with a company.

In particular, you should be aware of the following terms:

-   **Transfer of rights to the company from the student**: consider which rights you give the company to your paper, your results and products you made, if any, and if you can accept it.
-   **Stipulations on CBS and CBS' supervisors**: neither CBS nor CBS' employees can take part in an agreement on confidentiality between students and companies. Clauses regarding CBS and/or CBS' employees must therefore be removed, including signature lines.
-   **Transfer of rights to the company from CBS**: As a public institution, CBS cannot transfer any rights to a company without being paid according to the market price.

# GenAI at CBS

GenAI stands for _generative artificial intelligence_. It is a type of artificial intelligence based on large language models that can transform your written inputs into texts, images, videos and many other types of content. Well-known chatbots like Copilot and ChatGPT are built on GenAI. They produce content based on your prompts - written inputs - and you've probably already had some experience using it. To better understand what GenAI is, you can compare it to traditional AI:

- Traditional AI creates content solely based on your specific input.
- GenAI creates content that may be influenced by many different inputs from other users. Therefore, the content you create with GenAI is not entirely your own product.

## Where is GenAI used?

GenAI is used in many types of software - including Copilot and ChatGPT. But it can also be built into other types of software. For example, Grammarly has a built-in GenAI feature to do spell-checking. Make sure that GenAI features are turned off in situations where it is not permitted.

## Think about the climate when using GenAI

GenAI can make parts of your studies easier - but it certainly isn't free in terms of climate impact. When you ask a question to Copilot, it emits an amount of CO2 equivalent to charging a smartphone 40 times. That means it leaves a significant negative footprint on the climate. CBS encourages you to think carefully before using artificial intelligence. Consider whether other alternatives might be used instead - such as using a regular search engine like Google or finding pictures in a photo database.

## Use Copilot - for your safety

When you log in to Copilot with your CBS profile, your data is protected. Copilot safeguards your data so that no one but you can see what you type in. Copilot never uses your conversations to train the underlying GenAI model. With Copilot, you can be confident that personal or confidential information won't end up in the wrong hands.

## Be critical

GenAI-generated content is not always accurate, factually correct, or of high quality. That's why you should always:

- Double-check facts from reliable sources
- Use your academic judgment when evaluating content
- Avoid copying content directly without understanding or verifying it
- Speak to your supervisor when in doubt

## Protect your data

Unlike Copilot, ChatGPT by default uses your data and chat history to train itself to generate content. You can disable this in the app's settings. Be aware that it is your own responsibility to ensure that your chosen GenAI tools handle your data securely.

Checklist:

- Always check the settings in your programs
- Pay attention to GenAI icons in your software
- Ask your supervisor or instructor if you're unsure

## Rules about GenAI

### New GenAI rules at CBS from autumn semester 2025

Starting from the autumn semester 2025, CBS introduces new rules when it comes to the use of GenAI. The new rules allow you to use GenAI for most types of examinations in mandatory courses and for final projects. However, you are still not allowed to use GenAI for written sit-in exams in CBS Examination Halls. The new rules only apply to courses that begin 1 September 2025 or later. Courses that started before this date follow the previous guidelines.

### The new rules only apply to mandatory courses

For now, the new rules only apply to mandatory courses and mandatory courses offered as electives. Starting with the autumn semester 2026, the new rules will apply to all courses, no matter the type.

## GenAI in exams

It is important that you check what rules for GenAI apply to your exam. This is because different rules apply depending on whether your course is a mandatory course or an elective.

### GenAI for exams in mandatory courses

You **are allowed** to use GenAI for exams and final projects in mandatory courses and mandatory courses offered as electives. However, you are **not allowed** to use GenAI for written sit-in exams held in CBS Examination Halls. This applies no matter what type of aids you are allowed to use for the exam. You are also **not allowed** to use GenAI if the lecturer has decided so. If this is the case, it is written in your course description. Make sure you check the course description to avoid exam cheating.

### GenAI for exams in electives

You are **not allowed** to use GenAI for exams in electives. However, some lecturers may choose to allow the use of GenAI anyways. If this is the case, it is written in the course description. Make sure you check the course description to avoid exam cheating.

## Permitted use of GenAI

If you are allowed to use GenAI in your exam, you may use it for:

- Proofreading and language assistance - which may be built in into various programs
- Searching for knowledge - similar to using a regular search engine, just more advanced
- Idea generation and planning - e.g. coming up with good topics or managing your time

### GenAI and oral exams

If you use GenAI to prepare for an oral exam, you must disclose this at the exam. However, you are not allowed to use GenAI to generate answers during the oral exam itself. Similar to when you are citing sources, remember: You must always declare GenAI-generated content.

**Please note:** These rules only apply to mandatory courses and mandatory courses taken as electives that take place in the fall semester of 2025 or later.

### GenAI at lectures

You are allowed to use GenAI in your classes, as long as you follow CBS's guidelines for teaching activities. If the teaching activity is part of an exam, please refer to the guidelines for GenAI in exams.

In class, you can use GenAI for tasks such as:

- Brainstorming
- Structuring notes
- Getting an overview of the curriculum
- Creating personal study plans
- Explaining concepts

**Please note:** If you want to use GenAI to record teaching sessions, you must use Copilot only, as other types of software may violate CBS's Rules for recording classes.

### Be careful not to break the law

Even though GenAI can be of great help in your studies, you must be aware of the materials you share in the process. It is not legal to upload study materials, all or parts of syllabus or similar to e.g. ChatGPT, because it is copyright infringement. You are, however, allowed to share links to materials with your chosen GenAI, since it can only access the materials if it is already publicly available.

## Declaring GenAI-generated content

You must declare your use of GenAI in most instances. This is similar to how you are required to cite other sources and tools when using them in your academic work.

- For written exams, you must declare your use of GenAI in your methods section. If there is no methods section, you should do so in the introduction.
- For the preparation of oral exams, you must inform your lecturer, supervisor, or examiner that you have used GenAI. However, you are not allowed to use GenAI during the actual exam, for example, to generate a response. The same rule applies to oral defenses of final projects.
- You do not need to declare use of GenAI if you have only used it as a search engine or to proofread.

If you use GenAI-generated content without declaring it properly, it is considered exam cheating, which is a violation of CBS regulations. You can read more under Exam cheating.

The library has created a guide that explains how to declare your use of GenAI: [CBS Library's guide on declaring GenAI](https://libguides.cbs.dk/c.php?g=684990&p=5306399)

Contact your supervisor or the library if you are unsure how to use or declare GenAI in connection with an exam or your project/thesis.
